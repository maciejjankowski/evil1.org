What is Recursive Capitalism?

Recursive capitalism is the phenomenon where wealth and power concentrate in ever-tightening cycles. It's not just about rich people getting richer - it's about the system itself becoming more efficient at extracting value while becoming less accountable to democratic oversight.

Key characteristics of recursive capitalism include:

  • Regulatory Capture: Industries write their own rules through lobbying and revolving-door employment
  • Board Interlocks: Corporate directors serve on multiple boards, creating incestuous relationships
  • Political Donations: Campaign financing that ensures favorable legislation
  • Media Consolidation: News organizations owned by the same corporations they cover
  • Supply Chain Exploitation: Global networks that maximize profit while minimizing accountability
